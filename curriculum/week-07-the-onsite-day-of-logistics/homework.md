## Problem 1 — Add the onsite section to the screen-call cheat sheet (60 min)

Extend the cheat sheet from Weeks 4-6 with an onsite section that lives on the same single screen.

Open `c13-week-06/screen-call-cheat-sheet-v4.md` (or the most-recent version from the Week 6 homework). Add an onsite section. If you are keeping the cheat sheet per-week, start `c13-week-07/screen-call-cheat-sheet-v5.md` from the v4 version.

The new onsite section structure:

```markdown
## Onsite additions

### Pre-loop checklist (night before)

- Sleep: 8h target; light dinner; no alcohol; screens off 21:30
- Clothing laid out: ___ (specific outfit)
- Bag packed: laptop, charger, ID, water, snacks, scratch paper, pen, cheat sheet
- Transportation confirmed
- Recruiter's name and phone in my contacts
- Backup alarm set

### Morning of (2.5 hours before)

- Water 500ml; protein-heavy breakfast; usual caffeine
- 30-min warm-up: one easy LeetCode, Phase 1 only, narrated
- Read cheat sheet twice
- Arrive 15-20 min before start time
- Bathroom in lobby

### Between rounds (5-10 min)

- Water; bathroom (ask recruiter if needed)
- Walk with recruiter; do NOT relitigate previous round
- One slow breath x 2; one cheat-sheet glance
- "Round {N+1} starts now"

### Lunch interview

- Scored. Treat as a round.
- Order something normal, non-messy, eat moderately
- One specific question about the team's recent work ready: "I noticed ___; could you tell me more about how ___ was decided?"
- No complaining; no pumping for inside info

### Recruiter midpoint check

- Composed, specific, positive answer
- Pre-write: "Really good — {interviewer A's} round was {specific thing}; {interviewer B's} let me talk about {project}. I enjoyed the team."

### Hostile-question patterns (one phrase each)

1. Pushback: "Fair point. Let me reconsider — {options}."
2. Why this choice: name constraint, alternative, trade-off
3. Failure: real failure, my role, lesson applied since
4. What would you change: 2-3 specific things; 1 thing you'd keep
5. Interrupting: stop, acknowledge, pivot, check
6. Hypothetical hostile: clarify; walk steps; name principle
7. "Are you sure?": recheck; confirm with reasoning; hold position

### EEOC-protected categories — deflection one-liners

- Age / graduation year: "I have {X} years of relevant experience including..."
- Where from: "I'm based in {city} now. What I'd love to discuss is..."
- Religion / holidays: "Happy to work within whatever schedule the team uses."
- Marital: "I keep my personal life out of work conversations, but happy to talk about the role."
- Kids / family: "Not something I'd discuss in an interview. I'm fully committed to the role."
- Health / disability: "Nothing that would affect my ability to do the role. Specific physical requirement you wanted to clarify?"
- Genetic / family history: "Not something I'd discuss in an interview. Tell me more about the role."
- Citizenship: "I have legal right to work in the {country}. Specifics with HR if relevant to offer."
- Salary history: "I prefer not to anchor on previous comp. I'm targeting {range based on levels.fyi}."

### The closing with recruiter

- Composed answer to "how was your day?" — specific, positive, named
- Questions back: timeline, next step, comp range if not yet covered
- Closing line: "Thanks — really enjoyed the day. Looking forward to hearing."

### Banned phrases (onsite)

- "I'm so tired."
- "That last round was rough."
- "I'm not sure how that went."
- "This is so much harder than I expected."
- "Anyway."

### The bathroom rule

- Ask once per loop, minimum. If I haven't asked by minute 90, ask on the next walk.
```

**Acceptance:**

- The onsite section is added (or v5 cheat sheet exists).
- All sub-sections populated with my specific details (clothing, breakfast, deflection wording, banned phrases).
- Total cheat-sheet length still fits on a single laptop screen at normal zoom — under ~600 words including all of Weeks 4, 5, 6's content.
- Top of file has a date stamp; the cheat sheet revises after every real loop.

## Problem 6 — Week-7 reflection (45 min)

`c13-week-07/reflection.md`, 400-500 words:

1. Before this week, what was your model of the onsite loop? Be honest: did you think it was a longer version of the phone screen, or did you have a clear picture of how the multi-hour-with-multiple-interviewers dynamic changed what was being tested? Where was your model wrong?

2. Of the three exercises this week, which one moved the needle most, and why? (Most candidates report Exercise 2 — the deflection rehearsal — because the gap between "I read the deflection scripts" and "I can produce one in real time when a peer asks me 'do you have kids?' in roleplay" is larger than they expected.)

3. The mini-project mock revealed something about your pacing or your energy management across four hours that solo prep would not have surfaced. What was it, and what does that tell you about the limits of solo prep for the onsite specifically?

4. Lecture 3 covers the legal frame around hostile and illegal interview questions. Read your own deflection scripts after a week of rehearsal: are they fluent now, or do they still feel rehearsed? Which specific category most needs more rehearsal? What specifically would you do to close the gap before your next real loop?

5. The after-action template you built this week is a forever-artefact — it will be your post-loop document for the rest of your engineering career. What one structural choice in your template are you most proud of? What one structural choice are you least sure about, and how will you decide whether to keep it after the next real loop?

6. What one habit are you carrying into Week 8 (system design and senior-level rounds) from this week's work? Concrete, testable, not aspirational. (Example: "Before every prep session, including system-design prep, I will start with the 30-minute warm-up routine — one easy LeetCode Phase 1 narration — to keep the voice habit alive even when the content shifts.")
