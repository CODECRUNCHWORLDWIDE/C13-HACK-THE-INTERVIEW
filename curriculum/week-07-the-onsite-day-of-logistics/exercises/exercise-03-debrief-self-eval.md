# Exercise 3 — Debrief Self-Eval

**Time:** ~60 minutes (run within 2 hours of the mini-project's 4-hour mock OR a real onsite). **Output:** A scored self-evaluation of the loop you just completed — every round, every gap, the lunch, the closing, the read of the room across the day. Lives in your portfolio as `c13-week-07/debrief-self-eval-{date}.md`.

## Goal

Run the candidate-side version of the debrief that the company will run in 24-48 hours. The exercise produces a structured artefact that names what happened in the loop, scores it against the rubric, identifies the three to five issues most worth fixing, and feeds the after-action template (Challenge 2) and the run-of-show revision for the next loop.

The exercise is run within 2 hours of the loop ending — earlier if possible. The window is non-negotiable. By 12 hours after the loop, the granular memory of each round has decayed enough that the self-eval becomes generic; by 48 hours, the document is half-fiction.

## Setup

You need:

- **A loop to debrief.** Real (a recent onsite) or the mini-project's 4-hour mock from this week.
- **The run-of-show document** from Exercise 1 (or the equivalent for the real loop).
- **Your notes from the day** — anything you wrote down between rounds, during the day, or in the recruiter walks.
- **A markdown file**: `c13-week-07/debrief-self-eval-{date}.md`. Template below.
- **A quiet hour.** This is not multitasking work. The phone is on Do Not Disturb. The kitchen timer is set for 60 minutes.

## Process

### Step 1 — Header and overview (5 min)

```markdown
# Debrief self-eval — {company / mock}, {date}

## Header

- **Loop:** ___ (company name; "mini-project mock"; or other)
- **Date and time:** ___
- **Mode:** in-person / virtual
- **Total length:** ___ hours
- **Rounds completed:** ___ of ___ scheduled
- **Run-of-show file:** ___ (link)

## Overall read

In two sentences: was this loop a hire, lean hire, lean no-hire, no-hire,
from my read of it? On what evidence?

(Be honest. The temptation is to write "I think it went well" because that
is what you want to believe. The exercise has no value if it is reassurance.
The exercise has value if it names what actually happened.)

Two sentences: ___
```

### Step 2 — Per-round scoring (25 min)

For each round in the loop, fill out:

```markdown
## Round {N} — {type} — {start time}-{end time}

### Interviewer

- **Name:** ___
- **Title:** ___ (if known)
- **Apparent style:** ___ (warm / dry / hostile-by-design / hostile-by-accident
  / tired / engaged / decided-early / decided-late)

### The problem / prompt

- **What they asked:** ___ (verbatim if you remember; paraphrased otherwise)
- **How I'd categorise it:** easy / medium / hard; or behavioural / system
  design / coding / domain-specific

### What I did well

- One specific thing, named concretely. Not "communication"; "I asked the
  duplicates clarifying question before coding, which led to the cleaner
  hash-map approach."

### What I did poorly

- One to three specific things. Not "I could have done better"; "I went
  silent for 3 minutes during the optimisation phase; the interviewer's
  follow-up suggested they had lost the thread."

### The interviewer's apparent engagement

- 5 min mark: ___
- 15 min mark: ___
- 25 min mark: ___
- 35 min mark: ___
- Closing: ___

### Was the interviewer decided early?

- Yes / no / unclear: ___
- If yes, positively or negatively: ___
- The signals that tell me: ___

### Hostile or illegal moments

- Hostile pattern observed (which of the seven): ___
- Illegal question asked: yes / no
- If yes, category and the verbatim question: ___
- My response: ___
- My self-grade on the deflection (1-5): ___

### Score by dimension (1-5)

- **Problem-solving / structure:** __
- **Execution / technical content:** __
- **Communication / narration:** __
- **Composure under pressure:** __
- **Closing:** __
- **Total:** __ / 25

### My estimate of the interviewer's likely write-up

In one paragraph, write what I think this interviewer is writing in their
debrief notes about me. Be honest. The temptation is to write what I hope
they're writing; write what I think they're writing.

___
```

Repeat for every round of the loop. A four-round loop produces four of these. The lunch interview gets its own version with adapted dimensions (treat the peer engineer's "would I want to work with this person" as the central dimension).

## Acceptance criteria

- The file `c13-week-07/debrief-self-eval-{date}.md` exists and is committed.
- Every round in the loop has a per-round section, fully populated.
- The cross-round patterns section is populated with specific numbers (energy, narration coverage, breaks).
- The three-to-five-issues section names concrete, revisable habits — not aspirations.
- The cheat-sheet revisions name specific lines (old → new).
- The forward-looking note exists and is one paragraph, not one sentence.
- The exercise was completed within 24 hours of the loop ending; ideally within 2 hours.

## Why this exercise matters

The 24-48 hours after the loop is when the recruiter, the interviewers, and the debrief panel are scoring you. You cannot influence their scoring during that window. You can, however, score yourself in parallel, and the artefact you produce is the input to your next loop — which, in any normal cycle, is within 1-3 weeks.

The cost of not running this exercise: every loop produces about 40% of the learning available from it. The interviewers' decisions teach you something (whether you get the offer or not); the rounds' specific moments teach you ten times more, but only if you capture them while they are fresh.

The candidates who run this self-eval after every real loop converge to a much higher loop-success rate over the first 3-5 loops than candidates who do not. The difference is not technical preparation; it is the metacognitive feedback loop, which only exists if you write the artefact.

## When the loop is the mini-project mock, not a real loop

If you are running this exercise after the Week 7 mini-project (4-hour peer mock), the structure is the same with these adaptations:

- The "interviewer" in each round is the peer; their write-up is the peer-feedback record from the mock.
- The "recruiter" role does not exist in the mock; skip Section 4 or substitute "the rest of the cohort observing the mock."
- The "closing" is the cohort debrief, not a real recruiter closing.
- The "next loop" is your next mock or your first real loop, whichever comes first.

The self-eval is more valuable after the mock than after the real loop, because the mock is the dress rehearsal and the self-eval is what makes the dress rehearsal stick.
