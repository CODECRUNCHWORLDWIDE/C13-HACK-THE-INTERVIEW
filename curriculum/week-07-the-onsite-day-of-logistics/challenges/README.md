# Week 7 — Challenges

Two challenges. One sources the interviewer-side view of the loop; one builds the reusable artefact you fill out after every loop for the rest of the cycle.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Shadow a real loop](challenge-01-shadow-a-real-loop.md)** — draft, send, and track an informational request to a working engineer asking to be walked through the loop from the interviewer side (~60 min for the sending; the conversation, if accepted, is bonus)
2. **[Challenge 2 — After-action template](challenge-02-after-action-template.md)** — build a reusable, sub-30-minute after-action template and test it against the mini-project mock or a recent loop (~75 min)

Both are optional but high-leverage, and they sit on opposite sides of the loop. Challenge 1 is preparation: the interviewer-side perspective is the single largest information asymmetry in your prep, and the challenge is graded on the *sending* of a thoughtful request, not on the *receiving* of a yes. Challenge 2 is process: the after-action template is the artefact you fill out within 2 hours of every real onsite for the remainder of the cycle, and the version you build this week is the v1 you refine across loops.

Challenge 2 pairs directly with the exercises. The template is a leaner, faster version of Exercise 3's debrief self-eval, and the run-of-show from Exercise 1 names the exact post-loop slot where the after-action gets filled out. Do Exercise 3 first so you know the full-length shape; then build Challenge 2's template as the speed-tuned version you will actually use under fatigue.

Challenge 1 is a useful drop-in any week of the rest of the cycle — the candidates who send 5-8 informational requests across a job search average 1-2 high-leverage conversations, and each one produces interviewer-side detail unavailable from any public source.
