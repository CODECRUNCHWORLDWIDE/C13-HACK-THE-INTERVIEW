# Week 9 — Exercises

Three isolation drills. Each takes one phase of the live coding round out of context and reps it on its own, so the muscle builds without the rest of the round competing for attention.

1. **[Exercise 1 — The Clarification Drill](./exercise-01-the-clarification-drill.md)** — three 8-minute rounds on the clarify phase only, no code, the highest-leverage 3 minutes rehearsed in isolation (~30 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — The Stuck-Recovery Drill](./exercise-02-the-stuck-recovery-drill.md)** — a recorded mock on a problem one notch above your comfort level, with the hint button removed, so you exercise the three recovery moves on a real wall (~45 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — The Complexity-Defence Drill](./exercise-03-the-complexity-defence-drill.md)** — six 4-minute rounds analysing unfamiliar snippets on the spot: state the complexity, state whether it's optimal, name one trade-off (~30 min)

## How to work them

Do them in order. Exercise 1 trains the opening you'll lean on in every round; Exercise 2 trains the move that decides the round when it goes sideways; Exercise 3 trains the moment the interviewer most often probes.

Every one of these is a speak-out-loud drill. Reading the steps silently builds nothing — the whole point is the narration overhead, the same overhead you carry in the real round. Record yourself where the exercise asks for it and watch the recording back; that's where the reps compound.

When you've finished all three, check your work against **[SOLUTIONS.md](./SOLUTIONS.md)** — sample strong responses written as transcripts, with the inner-monologue narration that earns a passing-or-better signal. Grade your own recordings against the structure of those samples; don't memorise the wording.
