# Week 1 — The Hiring Pipeline

> *Most engineers think interview prep = LeetCode. The 30% of candidates who run a structured pipeline get more offers than the 70% who don't, with the same technical skill.*

Welcome to **C13 · Hack the Interview**. While C2 teaches you to *solve interview problems*, C13 teaches you to *navigate the hiring process itself*: pipeline math, target-list construction, recruiter conversations, offer evaluation, negotiation, and post-offer mechanics. By Sunday you have a working spreadsheet, a target list of 30 companies, and a recruiter outreach template you've actually sent.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

- **Map** the full hiring pipeline: cold outreach → recruiter screen → tech screen → onsite → offer → negotiation → decision.
- **Estimate** your funnel math: at any company, what's the conversion at each stage?
- **Build** a structured **target list** of ~30 companies tiered into reaches / matches / safeties — like college applications, but engineered.
- **Write** outreach messages that read as authored by a person, not a template — and that you'd actually send.
- **Track** the entire pipeline in a single spreadsheet that becomes your operating dashboard for the cycle.
- **Identify** the first signal of a "false positive" recruiter or a misaligned role within 10 minutes of a screen.
- **Distinguish** an authentic culture-fit conversation from "we don't know if we can pay you what you want yet."

## Prerequisites

- You have either completed **C2 · CrunchTime The Code** or are running it in parallel. C13 assumes you can solve interview problems; C13 teaches the *process around them*.
- You have a **resume that exists** (it can be bad — Week 4 fixes it).
- You have a **public GitHub profile** with at least one project visible. If not, do [C3](../C3-CRUNCH-LABS-PORTFOLIO/) first.
- You have **20–30 hours/week** for ~10 weeks. Less than that, your funnel runs cold.

## Topics covered

- The hiring pipeline as a system, not a sequence of events
- Funnel math: realistic conversion rates at each stage
- The "30 / 60 / 90" target list (30 companies, 60 humans, 90 minutes/week of outreach)
- The roles of recruiter, hiring manager, hiring committee
- Anatomy of a recruiter screen vs. a hiring-manager screen
- Outreach: cold (cold-emailing into HR) vs. warm (referrals from your network)
- Application Tracking Systems (ATS) and the keyword game
- Why "I applied online and got ghosted" usually means the ATS filtered you, not the company
- Recruiter incentives: how they're compensated and what that means for you
- The spreadsheet (your single source of truth for the cycle)
- Pacing: how to run 3–4 active processes simultaneously without burning out
