# Week 1 — Quiz

Ten questions. Lectures closed.

---

**Q1.** Typical conversion from cold ATS application to recruiter call (at a large company) is closest to:

- A) 1-5%
- B) 15-25%
- C) 30-50%
- D) 50-70%

---

**Q2.** Which outreach mode has the highest conversion to a recruiter call?

- A) Cold LinkedIn DM to recruiter
- B) Warm referral from a friend at the company
- C) Cold application via the careers page
- D) Recruiter-initiated outreach

---

**Q3.** A recruiter asks for your comp expectations on the first 30-min call. The best move is:

- A) Quote the lowest you'd accept (to seem reasonable).
- B) Quote your dream number (to anchor high).
- C) Defer politely: "I'd like to learn more about the level and role first."
- D) Refuse to discuss comp at all.

---

**Q4.** The steepest drop in a typical hiring funnel is at the:

- A) Outreach → recruiter call stage.
- B) Recruiter → hiring-manager stage.
- C) Tech screen → onsite stage.
- D) Onsite → offer stage.

---

**Q5.** A "Tier-A" (Reach) company is best described as:

- A) A company you'd take only if everything else fails.
- B) A stretch but realistic company where comp is at the top of your band.
- C) Any FAANG company, automatically.
- D) Any company that publishes blog posts.

---

**Q6.** Why is a two-column resume risky?

- A) Recruiters consider it visually cluttered.
- B) Most ATSes parse left-to-right and produce interleaved nonsense.
- C) PDF readers don't support two columns.
- D) It signals you're junior.

---

**Q7.** Of your 30-company target list, how many should ideally have a 1st-degree contact?

- A) 0
- B) 2-3
- C) 8-12
- D) All 30

---

**Q8.** An *external* (agency) recruiter is typically compensated:

- A) Flat fee per submitted candidate, regardless of outcome.
- B) Hourly, like a consultant.
- C) A percentage of the candidate's first-year base, paid by the company, only if the candidate accepts.
- D) Only by the candidate.

---

**Q9.** A target Tier-A company has not replied to your outreach in 5 business days. Best move:

- A) Send a daily follow-up until they reply.
- B) Mark as cold and never contact again.
- C) Send one polite follow-up referencing the original message; if no reply in 7 more days, move on.
- D) Apply through their careers page in parallel.

---

**Q10.** A common Week-1 mistake is:

- A) Setting up the tracking spreadsheet too early.
- B) Sending the first outreach before drafting and reviewing it.
- C) Researching comp on levels.fyi.
- D) Adding "First-degree contact" as a column.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal after attempting</summary>

1. **A** — 1-5%. ATS is a numbers game and most cold applications never reach a human.
2. **B** — Warm referrals, ~30-50% conversion.
3. **C** — Defer politely. Day-1 anchoring on comp almost always costs you money.
4. **D** — Onsite to offer is the steepest drop (typically 25-45% conversion).
5. **B** — Top-of-band, selective, stretch-but-realistic.
6. **B** — ATS parsers usually choke on two-column layouts.
7. **C** — 8-12 of 30. Less than that and your funnel is too cold.
8. **C** — Percent of first-year base, paid by company, contingent on accept.
9. **C** — One polite follow-up, then move on. Daily follow-ups burn the relationship.
10. **B** — Sending bad outreach burns relationships you'll want later. Draft first, sit on it, then send.

</details>

If under 7, re-read the lecture you missed. If 9+, you're ready for the homework.
