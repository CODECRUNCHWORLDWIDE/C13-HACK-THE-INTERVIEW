# Mini-Project — The Tracking Dashboard

> Your operating dashboard for the rest of the cycle. By end of Week 1, you should be looking at it every morning.

**Estimated time:** 5-7 hours, spread across Thursday-Saturday.

## What you ship

A **single dashboard** (Google Sheet, Airtable, or `.md` file) containing:

1. The 30-company tracking spreadsheet from Exercise 1 + 2, fully populated.
2. A **funnel summary** view: count of companies in each stage.
3. A **this-week action list**: for each row with a "next action" within the next 7 days, the action.
4. A **comp research tab** with the data from Homework Problem 2.
5. A **first outreach sent** record with date, recipient, and the final message (Homework Problem 3).
6. **5 outreach drafts** from Exercise 3 (not yet sent — these get reviewed on Sunday of Week 2).
7. A **README at the top** explaining what each tab/section is and how to use it.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Dashboard URL or file path recorded in your portfolio's `c13-week-01/mini-project/README.md`.
- [ ] 30 companies fully populated.
- [ ] Funnel summary count is computed (not manual).
- [ ] This-week action list is current as of the date you commit.
- [ ] Comp tab has at least Tier-A research complete.
- [ ] First outreach is logged with the message text and date.
- [ ] At least one person has reviewed the dashboard (a peer, a mentor, or yourself 24 hours later with fresh eyes) and you've applied at least one piece of their feedback.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight | "Great" looks like |
|-----------|------:|--------------------|
| Completeness | 30% | All 30 companies fully populated |
| Honesty of tiering | 20% | Tiers actually reflect different effort/reward, not all A |
| Comp research depth | 15% | Tier-A has researched bands, not "I think they pay well" |
| Outreach quality | 15% | Drafts pass the 60-second test |
| Usability | 10% | You'd actually look at this every morning |
| First action taken | 10% | One real outreach sent |

## How to set up the rhythm

Once the dashboard is live, build a **5-minute daily check**:

- Morning: open the dashboard. What's my next action today?
- Friday: do the "weekly review" — for every row, is the stage still accurate? Is the next-action date still right?
- Every Sunday of every week: re-read your own funnel projection from Challenge 2. Compare against actuals.

This rhythm is what separates candidates who stay on top of their cycle from candidates who lose track at week 5.

## Stretch

- Build a **timeline chart** showing how each company has moved through stages over time (Google Sheets has decent charting).
- Add **calendar integration** — when a stage moves, a Google Calendar event is created for the follow-up.
- Share **a redacted version** of the dashboard with peers in your study group as a template.

## Why this matters

Of every job-search artifact you'll produce this cycle, this dashboard has the highest leverage. It's the difference between running your search and your search running you. By Week 8, the version you have now will look primitive — but every iteration starts from this Week-1 baseline.
