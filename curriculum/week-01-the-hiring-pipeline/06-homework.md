# Week 1 — Homework

Six problems, ~6 hours total. Each commits to your portfolio.

---

## Problem 1 — Identify your network (45 min)

Open LinkedIn (or your equivalent). For each of your 30 target companies, search whether any 1st-degree connection works there.

**Acceptance:**

- `network-audit.md` in your portfolio, listing for each company: whether you have a 1st-degree contact (yes/no), name (initials OK if public), and a 1-line note on the relationship strength.
- A summary count: "X of 30 have a 1st-degree contact."

---

## Problem 2 — Comp research deep-dive (60 min)

For each Tier-A company, look up the comp band on levels.fyi (or equivalent) for the specific level you're targeting.

**Acceptance:**

- `comp-research.md` with a row per Tier-A company: company, level you're targeting, base range, equity range, total compensation range, source URL.
- Three sentences identifying which Tier-A is the comp outlier (highest), and which is the lowest. Don't share this publicly; it goes into your private notes.

---

## Problem 3 — One actual outreach sent (75 min)

Take one of your five drafts from Exercise 3. Edit it. Sit with it for 24 hours. Then actually send it.

**Acceptance:**

- The message sent (a copy of the final version saved in `first-outreach-sent.md`).
- The date/time it was sent and to whom.
- A line on what you'd change if you were writing it from scratch now.

---

## Problem 4 — Update LinkedIn (45 min)

Your LinkedIn profile is a public resume. Get it to a baseline.

**Acceptance:**

- A professional headshot (or competent placeholder).
- Headline that reflects what you ARE, not just where you work. ("Backend engineer focused on $thing" beats "Software Engineer at $company.")
- Each role lists 3-5 bullets focused on outcomes ("Reduced p99 latency 40%") not duties.
- A short "About" section (3-5 sentences).
- All of this committed to `linkedin-checklist.md` confirming each was done.

---

## Problem 5 — Resume baseline (75 min)

Pull your current resume. Apply the basic ATS-friendly checklist:

- Single column.
- Standard sections (Education, Experience, Skills, Projects).
- PDF, exported from a text source.
- No graphics in the header.
- Date format consistent (e.g., "Jan 2024 – Present").
- Length: 1 page (junior) or 1-2 pages (senior+).

**Acceptance:**

- A `resume-v1.pdf` committed to your portfolio.
- `resume-checklist.md` confirming each rule above.
- An ATS parser test (paste into <https://resume-magic.app/> or similar): screenshot the output.

(Week 4 of C13 does a deeper resume rewrite. This is just the baseline.)

---

## Problem 6 — Week-1 reflection (45 min)

`reflection.md`, 300-400 words:

1. Which Tier did you find hardest to fill, and why?
2. What surprised you about the comp research?
3. What part of the outreach process feels most uncomfortable to you? Be specific.
4. What's one thing you want to do differently in Week 2?
