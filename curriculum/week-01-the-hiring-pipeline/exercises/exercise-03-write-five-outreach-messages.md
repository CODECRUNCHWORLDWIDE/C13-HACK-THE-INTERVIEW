# Exercise 3 — Write five outreach messages

**Time:** ~60 min. **Output:** Five drafted (but not sent) outreach messages, varied by context.

## Goal

Practice the **specific-and-personal** outreach writing voice on five different contexts. **Do not send any of them this week.** Sending bad outreach burns relationships. Drafting and reviewing is the lesson.

## The five messages

Write all five into a single file: `c13-week-01/outreach-drafts.md`. Each is to a real company from your Exercise-2 list.

### 1. Warm referral request

You have a 1st-degree contact (a friend / former colleague) at a Tier-A company. You're asking them to refer you.

Target length: 100-150 words. Should include:

- Acknowledge your relationship.
- Specify the role/team you'd like to apply to.
- Make the ask easy ("if you're comfortable, would you be willing to forward my resume to $person?").
- Make declining easy ("no pressure if not — I know referrals are a real ask").
- Attach resume + 1-line context.

### 2. Cold LinkedIn message to a recruiter

You don't have a contact. You found the recruiter for a role at a Tier-B company. Cold message.

Target length: 80-120 words. Should include:

- Reference the specific role.
- One sentence on why you (specific tech / experience overlap).
- One sentence demonstrating you know what the company does.
- Bounded ask ("would there be 20 minutes…").

### 3. Cold email to a hiring manager

You found a hiring manager's email through their conference talk or blog. They have NOT posted a public job opening.

Target length: 100-150 words. Should include:

- Reference what they wrote / built (be specific).
- One sentence of overlap with their work.
- The fact that you'd be interested if they're hiring or hiring-soon.
- Make declining graceful.

### 4. Follow-up to a message that didn't get a reply

A recruiter at a Tier-B company you messaged 5 business days ago hasn't replied.

Target length: 40-60 words. Should include:

- A short reference to the prior message.
- One *new* piece of value or detail (a recent project, a clarifying point).
- A way out for them ("totally fine if the timing isn't right").

### 5. "Reaching out after a no" — soft re-engagement

You interviewed at a Tier-A company 6 months ago, got rejected at the onsite. Now you're back in the market.

Target length: 80-120 words. Should include:

- Acknowledge the prior process gracefully (no chip on shoulder).
- One specific thing you've done since that's relevant (a project, a year of growth, a published talk).
- Ask if they have appropriate openings now.

## Workflow for each message

1. Pick the company and the actual recipient (real names if possible — research who you'd be writing to).
2. Write a first draft. Don't optimize yet.
3. Wait 10 minutes. Re-read out loud.
4. **Run the 60-second test:** could this exact message be sent to 5 other companies? If yes, rewrite.
5. Edit for length. Tight is good.
6. Re-read for tone: friendly but not chummy, specific but not desperate.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] `outreach-drafts.md` contains all 5 messages, each clearly labeled by context.
- [ ] Each message is within the target length.
- [ ] Each names a specific company and (where applicable) a specific person.
- [ ] Each passes the 60-second test — no template-shaped phrases like "passionate about technology" or "dynamic team."
- [ ] You wrote a 100-word reflection at the bottom: "Which was hardest to write, and why?"

## Important — do NOT send these messages this week

Sit on them for one full week. Re-read on Sunday of Week 2. **You will be embarrassed by at least one.** That's data; revise it. THEN send.

## Stretch

- Have a peer (from C2 or your study group) read your messages and tell you which ONE phrase in each makes them roll their eyes. Replace those phrases.
- Read 5 cold messages you've received recently. Note what made you respond or ignore each.
