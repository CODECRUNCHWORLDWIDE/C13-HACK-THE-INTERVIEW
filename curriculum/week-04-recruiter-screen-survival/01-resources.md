# Week 4 — Resources

Free, public, no signup unless noted.

## Compensation data

- **levels.fyi** — the canonical public compensation database for tech. Filter by company, level, location. Includes base, equity, bonus, and signing breakdowns:
  <https://www.levels.fyi/>
- **levels.fyi — "End of year report"** (annual, free, summarises bands by level across the industry):
  <https://www.levels.fyi/blog/2024-end-of-year-pay-report.html> *(replace year as new reports drop)*
- **levels.fyi — "How to negotiate tech salary"** (essay-length, the cleanest summary of the negotiation game):
  <https://www.levels.fyi/blog/how-to-negotiate-tech-salary>
- **H1B salary database — h1bdata.info** (public USCIS-disclosed offer letters, useful for ground-truth on base salaries):
  <https://h1bdata.info/>
- **salary.com / Glassdoor** — secondary references; less reliable than levels.fyi but useful for non-tech-pure roles and small companies:
  <https://www.salary.com/> · <https://www.glassdoor.com/>
- **Pay-transparency laws** — many US states (CA, CO, NY, WA, IL) and EU jurisdictions now require posted salary ranges. The job posting itself is increasingly the most-authoritative band reference. Read the JD's salary section before the screen.

## Glossary

| Term | One-line definition |
|------|---------------------|
| **Recruiter screen** | The first phone or video call in an interview loop, typically 25-35 minutes, run by a recruiter (not a hiring manager or engineer). |
| **Internal recruiter** | A salaried employee of the company hiring. Their incentive: fill the req at a number their boss approves. |
| **Agency recruiter** | A contractor paid a percentage of the candidate's first-year base by the hiring company. Incentive: close at the highest defensible number. |
| **Band** | The pre-approved compensation range for a given level at a company. Recruiter has a "low," a "target," and a "max." |
| **Base** | The annual cash salary, paid in regular paychecks. The most-discussed component because it is the most-comparable across companies. |
| **Total comp (TC)** | Base + equity (annualised) + signing bonus (annualised across vesting period) + annual cash bonus. The number levels.fyi reports. |
| **levels.fyi** | The dominant public compensation database for tech. Filter by company, level, location; reports percentiles. |
| **High anchor** | The first compensation number named in a negotiation, set deliberately at the top of your defensible band. Anchors the negotiation around your number, not theirs. |
| **Range vs. point** | A "range" is `$X to $Y`; a "point" is `$X`. You almost always give a range; the recruiter almost always asks you to make it a point. |
| **The flinch** | The recruiter's verbal reaction (or pause) when you name a number above their expected band. Tells you the number is in or above range. |
| **Notice period** | The number of weeks between you signing an offer and you actually starting. Recruiters ask early; have an answer ready. |
| **Work authorisation** | Your legal right to work in the country where the role is located. Recruiters ask early; honest one-sentence answer is required. |
| **The fit-and-friction filter** | The four-question test the recruiter screen runs: are you who your resume says, will you pass technical, do you want this role, is your comp in band? |
