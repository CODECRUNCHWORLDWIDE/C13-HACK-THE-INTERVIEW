# Week 4 — Exercises

Three exercises. Each operates on a specific beat of the recruiter screen.

1. **[Exercise 1 — Tell me about yourself](./exercise-01-tell-me-about-yourself.md)** — draft, time, and record your 2-3 minute self-intro until you can deliver it under 180 seconds (~60 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Why this company](./exercise-02-why-this-company.md)** — produce three-reason "why us" answers for five Tier-A targets (~90 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Comp research deep-dive](./exercise-03-comp-research-deep.md)** — use levels.fyi to build defensible comp bands for your top 5 targets (~90 min)

## How to work them

Do them in order. Exercise 1 is the most reused script of the entire cycle — you will deliver the self-intro answer in every screen you take, so it earns the most reps. Exercise 2 is per-company prep that compounds: once you've drafted five, the sixth takes 10 minutes instead of 20. Exercise 3 is the comp homework that decides which lever you anchor on per company and how much money you leave on the table.

**Don't claim recruiter-screen readiness until all three exercises and the mini-project recording are done.** A great self-intro with no comp prep gets you a great-feeling screen that ends with you under-anchoring by $40k. The combination is what wins.
