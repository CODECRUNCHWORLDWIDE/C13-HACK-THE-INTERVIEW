# Week 2 — Homework

Six problems, ~6 hours total. Each commits to your portfolio.

---

## Problem 1 — Audit your master resume's keyword coverage (45 min)

Open the five JDs you considered most carefully in Week 1 (from your Tier-A and Tier-B target lists). For each JD, extract the hard-skill keywords (15-25 per JD) and check whether each appears in your master resume.

**Acceptance:**

- `c13-week-02/keyword-audit.md` with one table per JD: JD term / In master? (Yes/No/Partial) / If "No," do I have this skill?
- A summary at the top: overall keyword match percentage across the five JDs, weighted by JD.
- A 100-word "what's missing" paragraph: which 3 skills would most improve your match score if added (truthfully) to the master?

---

## Problem 2 — Rewrite five more bullets (75 min)

Take five **more** weak bullets from your master (different from the five in Exercise 2). Rewrite each into STAR+R, in the same Before/After/What-changed format.

**Acceptance:**

- `c13-week-02/homework-bullet-rewrites.md` with the same structure as Exercise 2.
- Each After bullet contains at least one quantified result.
- Together with Exercise 2's five, you should have 10 rewritten bullets — bringing your overall number-density above 40% if you started below.

---

## Problem 3 — Parse-and-fix iteration (60 min)

Run your tailored Tier-A resume (from Exercise 3) through the ATS parser one more time. Compare the parser's output to your earlier diagnostic from Exercise 1.

**Acceptance:**

- `c13-week-02/parse-fix.md` listing each field that was wrong before and is now correct (or still wrong).
- If anything is still wrong, name the specific structural change you'd make to fix it.
- Apply at least one fix and re-run the parser. Note the result.

---

## Problem 4 — One full submission, real (75 min)

Pick **one** Tier-B company on your list with an active opening. Apply, using your tailored resume from Exercise 3 (the tailored version, not the master).

**Acceptance:**

- `c13-week-02/first-application-sent.md` containing:
  - Company name + role + JD link.
  - The tailored resume version submitted (copy in repo).
  - The cover letter (if required) or the application-question answers (if asked).
  - Date and time of submission.
  - The stage cell in your Week-1 tracking spreadsheet updated to "Outreach sent" or "Applied."

(Why Tier-B not Tier-A: practice on a real-but-lower-stakes application before you spend your Tier-A shots.)

---

## Problem 5 — Read three resumes from your target level (45 min)

Find three public resumes (personal websites, LinkedIn detailed view, GitHub-hosted PDFs) of engineers at the level you're targeting, ideally at companies on your list.

**Acceptance:**

- `c13-week-02/competitor-resumes.md` with three sections — one per resume — covering:
  - Link or source.
  - What's strong (specific bullets that landed, layout choices that work).
  - What's weak or what you'd do differently.
  - One pattern you're going to steal for your own v2.

---

## Problem 6 — Week-2 reflection (40 min)

`c13-week-02/reflection.md`, 300-400 words:

1. What was the most embarrassing thing your v1 resume did that you didn't notice until you ran the parser?
2. Which bullet was hardest to rewrite into a quantified outcome, and why?
3. If a recruiter had only your 30-second test results to go on, would they call you? Defend your answer.
4. What one habit are you going to carry into Week 3 — concrete, testable, not aspirational?
