# Week 2 — Exercises

Three exercises. Each operates on the actual resume you'll be submitting.

1. **[Exercise 1 — Parse your own resume](exercise-01-parse-your-own-resume.md)** — run your `resume-v1.pdf` through a parser and diagnose what's broken (~45 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Rewrite five bullets](exercise-02-rewrite-five-bullets.md)** — pick your weakest five bullets and STAR+R them (~90 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Tailor for three roles](exercise-03-tailor-for-three-roles.md)** — produce three tailored versions for three real JDs (~75 min)

## How to work them

Do them in order. Exercise 1 surfaces structural defects you'll want fixed *before* you spend 90 minutes rewriting bullets in Exercise 2. Exercise 3 stress-tests whether your rewritten bullets actually adapt to different JDs without further surgery.

**Don't ship a resume to a real company until you've done all three exercises and the mini-project peer review.** The point of this week is not "produce a resume." The point is "produce a resume you can defend in detail."
