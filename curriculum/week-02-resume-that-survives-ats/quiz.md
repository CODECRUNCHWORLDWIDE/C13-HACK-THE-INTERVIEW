# Week 2 — Quiz

Ten questions. Lectures closed.

---

**Q1.** When a PDF is "exported from a text source," what does that mean?

- A) The file size is under 1 MB.
- B) The PDF contains a selectable / extractable text layer (not just images).
- C) The PDF was generated by a printer.
- D) The PDF includes embedded fonts.

---

**Q2.** A two-column resume parses badly because:

- A) The ATS rejects any PDF with more than one column on principle.
- B) Most parsers read line-by-line left-to-right and produce interleaved nonsense across the columns.
- C) Two-column layouts are too short for senior candidates.
- D) PDF readers can't render two columns.

---

**Q3.** The recruiter's saved search runs `skills:Kubernetes`. Your resume says "container orchestration." The result is:

- A) You match — modern ATSes resolve synonyms.
- B) You don't match — the literal keyword has to appear.
- C) You match if the resume mentions Docker anywhere.
- D) The ATS asks the recruiter to manually verify.

---

**Q4.** Across your full resume, the recommended number-density target (bullets containing a quantified result) is approximately:

- A) ≥10%
- B) ≥40%
- C) ≥80%
- D) 100%

---

**Q5.** Which bullet shape is the "duties bullet" antipattern?

- A) "Reduced p99 latency from 1.4s to 220ms by sharding the index."
- B) "Responsible for backend services."
- C) "Cut CI pipeline duration from 22 minutes to 6 minutes."
- D) "Led the extraction of the billing service from the monolith."

---

**Q6.** In a bullet for a current (still-active) role, the correct tense is:

- A) Always past tense.
- B) Always present tense.
- C) Past tense for completed achievements, present tense for ongoing responsibilities.
- D) Future tense.

---

**Q7.** Date format consistency matters because:

- A) The ATS rejects any resume with inconsistent date formats.
- B) Mixed formats cause the parser to mis-extract one or more job dates, which can drop you below a "years of experience" filter.
- C) Recruiters dock points for stylistic inconsistency.
- D) Consistent dates rank higher in keyword search.

---

**Q8.** "Tailoring" a resume per JD means:

- A) Rewriting every bullet from scratch for the new role.
- B) Adjusting keyword emphasis, bullet ordering, and skills section to align with the JD — without changing factual content.
- C) Adding skills you don't have so the keyword match score improves.
- D) Removing all bullets that aren't 100% relevant.

---

**Q9.** A senior engineer's resume is 1.3 pages. The defensible advice is:

- A) Pad to 2 full pages so the resume looks senior.
- B) Cut to 1 page; a well-organized 1-pager beats a 2-pager with padding.
- C) Add a "cover letter" page to round out the document.
- D) Leave it; 1.3 is fine.

---

**Q10.** A common failure of the "header" section that hides the candidate's name from the parser is:

- A) Using a too-small font size.
- B) Placing the name inside a graphic / image element instead of as plain text.
- C) Forgetting to bold the name.
- D) Listing the name in the footer instead of the header.

---

## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal after attempting</summary>

1. **B** — Text-source PDFs have a selectable / extractable text layer; image-source PDFs do not, and parsers struggle with them.
2. **B** — Most parsers stream text left-to-right; two columns interleave.
3. **B** — Keyword matching is literal; synonyms don't count. Add the literal term (truthfully) if you have the skill.
4. **B** — ≥40% across the document is the target. 100% reads as performative.
5. **B** — "Responsible for X" describes scope, not outcome. A, C, D all contain specific quantified actions.
6. **C** — Past tense for completed achievements, present tense for ongoing responsibilities. Mixing tenses correctly is fine.
7. **B** — Inconsistent date formats cause parser failures that drop you below experience filters in the recruiter's search.
8. **B** — Adjust emphasis and keyword overlap without fabricating. A is wasteful, C is dishonest, D is over-aggressive.
9. **B** — A clean 1-pager wins. Padding to 2 dilutes the strongest material.
10. **B** — Names inside graphics or fancy header elements don't extract; the parser fails to identify the candidate.

</details>

If under 7, re-read the lecture you missed. If 9+, you're ready for the homework.
