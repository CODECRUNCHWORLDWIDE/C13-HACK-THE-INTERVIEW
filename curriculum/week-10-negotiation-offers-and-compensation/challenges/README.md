# Week 10 — Challenges

Two challenges. One builds the composure to walk away from a pressure tactic; one builds the relational discipline to close out the offers you decline without burning the bridge.

1. **[Challenge 1 — The exploding offer drill](challenge-01-the-exploding-offer.md)** — recognise the artificially short deadline, request the extension calmly, and write the walk-away script for the case where the recruiter refuses — the move that holds whether the deadline is real or manufactured (~45-60 min)
2. **[Challenge 2 — The decline-gracefully email](challenge-02-the-decline-email.md)** — write two decline emails, one to a process recruiter and one to a founder, calibrating warmth, specificity, and door-open phrasing to the depth of each relationship (~30-45 min)

Both are optional but high-leverage. Challenge 1 is composure under a deliberate pressure tactic: the exploding offer is a test, and the candidate who folds under the 48-hour deadline gets the worse comp while the candidate willing to walk often gets both the extension and a small bump. Challenge 2 is the most under-rehearsed move in the whole loop — the decline email — where a cold three-line note sent two days late burns a bridge the candidate did not realise was a bridge.

There is no SOLUTIONS.md for this week's challenges; the worked walk-away script and the example decline emails (strong and weak versions for contrast) live inside the challenge files themselves. Read your own draft first, then compare against the examples and note what you would change.

Run Challenge 1 first if a real offer is imminent — the willingness to walk is the capacity that fails live, and most candidates cannot bring themselves to write the walk-away script the first time, which is exactly the discomfort the drill exists to remove. Run Challenge 2 once you have offers to decline; the founder email is almost always the harder of the two because the relational debt is higher. Both produce a small artefact you keep on hand — a walk-away script, two decline drafts — that you adapt in the real situation rather than composing cold.
