# Week 10 Quiz — Final Exam Format

**12 questions. 60 minutes. Closed-book first pass; open-book second pass for any you missed.**

This is the C13 track's final-week quiz and is written in final-exam format — mixed short-answer, calculation, scenario response, and matching. Treat it as the calibration of where Week 10 has and has not landed. The quiz is also the calibration for the capstone mini-project; questions you struggle on are areas to revisit in the capstone narrative.

There are no separate "answers" appended to this quiz. The answers are in the lecture notes and resources. After your first pass, re-open the lectures for any question you could not answer, and write your own answer with citation to the lecture and section.

## Section A — Offer-letter literacy (3 questions)

### Q1. Vesting schedule arithmetic

You receive an offer with the following compensation:

- Base salary: $160,000
- Sign-on bonus: $30,000 (one-time, paid first paycheck, 12-month full claw-back)
- Stock grant: $280,000 over 4 years, vesting 25/25/25/25 with a 1-year cliff
- Target performance bonus: 12% of base

Compute, and show your work:

a. Year-1 total comp (sign-on included)
b. Year-2 total comp
c. Year-3 total comp
d. Year-4 total comp
e. Four-year total comp
f. Four-year average annual

Then compute the same numbers for an Amazon-style 5/15/40/40 schedule, assuming the same base, target bonus, and stock-grant total of $280k. Assume the Amazon offer also includes year-1 sign-on of $35k and year-2 sign-on of $25k to offset the back-loading.

### Q2. RSUs vs options

For each of the following statements, mark TRUE or FALSE. For any FALSE answers, write the corrected version.

a. RSUs at a public company are taxed as ordinary income at the moment of vest.
b. Options at a private company have a strike price equal to the company's most recent 409A valuation on the grant date.
c. The dollar value of an RSU grant on an offer letter is the vest-date value, not the grant-date value.
d. A 1-year cliff means that if you leave at month 11, you forfeit 100% of the unvested stock.
e. Amazon's standard 5/15/40/40 vesting schedule is offset by larger year-3 and year-4 sign-on cash payments.

### Q3. Negotiable vs non-negotiable lines

For each of the following lines on an offer letter, mark NEGOTIABLE, SOMETIMES NEGOTIABLE, or NEVER NEGOTIABLE. For each "sometimes" answer, name the condition under which it becomes negotiable.

a. Base salary
b. Signing bonus
c. Stock grant size
d. Vesting schedule (at Amazon specifically)
e. Target bonus percentage
f. Start date
g. At-will employment clause
h. Non-solicit clause
i. Non-compete scope (which companies count as competitors)
j. Arbitration clause
k. PTO accrual rate
l. Equity refresh policy

## Section B — Compensation reading (3 questions)

### Q4. levels.fyi calibration

You are evaluating an offer for a software engineer at Google L4 in Mountain View. The levels.fyi distribution for this triple, filtered to the last 24 months and after excluding 4 outliers, shows:

- n = 73 submissions
- 25th percentile total comp: $260k
- Median total comp: $295k
- 75th percentile total comp: $335k
- 90th percentile total comp: $380k

The offer is at $282k year-1 total comp.

a. At what percentile of the distribution is this offer? (Approximate.)
b. Is this offer "below market," "at market," or "above market" for the triple?
c. Name two reasons the median ($295k) might over-state or under-state the typical offer for this triple.
d. Compute the spread between the 25th and 75th percentile as a percentage of the median.

### Q5. BLS cross-check

You look up the BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics for "Software developers, applications" (SOC 15-1252) in the San Francisco-Oakland metro and find a median annual wage of $191,000 (cash, including bonus, not including stock).

a. What is the ratio of the Google L4 cash compensation (base + bonus only, approximately $185k at the median) to the BLS metro median?
b. What is the ratio of the Google L4 total compensation (including stock, $295k median) to the BLS metro median cash?
c. Interpret what these two ratios tell you about the structure of Google's L4 compensation versus the broader software-developer labour market in the metro.

### Q6. Cost-of-living adjustment

You are deciding between two offers:

- **Offer A:** San Francisco, $310k year-1 total comp.
- **Offer B:** Austin, $235k year-1 total comp.

NerdWallet's cost-of-living calculator says $310k in San Francisco is equivalent to approximately $195k in Austin in purchasing-power terms. Numbeo's index puts the same conversion at $210k.

a. Using the NerdWallet number, is Offer A or Offer B the higher offer in purchasing-power terms?
b. What state-income-tax adjustment should you apply? (California top rate vs Texas top rate.)
c. After the state-tax adjustment, which offer is higher?
d. Name three qualitative factors that the cost-of-living adjustment does not capture and that should be in the decision.

## Section D — Cost of living, decline gracefully, FTC ruling (3 questions)

### Q10. The decline-gracefully email

You completed the loop at Company X. You worked closely with Maya, the technical recruiter, who personally pulled some strings to accommodate a schedule conflict during your onsite. You have decided to accept an offer from Company Y instead. The decision was driven by the technical scope at Company Y.

The email should:

a. Acknowledge Maya's specific contribution.
b. Name the decision clearly.
c. Name Company Y by name (optional but recommended).
d. Give a specific reason for the decision.
e. Leave the door open for the future.
f. Close warmly without sounding hollow.

## Scoring

- 0-2 questions strongly answered: re-read all three lectures. Defer the mini-project until the lectures land.
- 3-6 questions strongly answered: re-read the lectures corresponding to your weakest section. Run the mini-project in parallel.
- 7-9 questions strongly answered: solid baseline. Proceed to the mini-project; re-visit weak areas during the capstone narrative.
- 10-12 questions strongly answered: strong baseline. Use the mini-project to integrate.

There is no passing score. The quiz is calibration, not certification. The strongest signal of readiness is the capstone mini-project, not the quiz.

## Reflection

After completing the quiz, write down:

1. **Which section was weakest?** Section A (offer-letter literacy), B (compensation reading), C (negotiation moves), or D (cost of living + decline + FTC + pre-signing).
2. **Of the 12 questions, which one most surprised you with your own answer?** The question where your answer revealed a gap you did not know you had is the question to re-read the lecture on.
3. **One question you would add to this quiz if you were writing it.** The act of writing the question forces you to articulate what you think the most important point of the week is.

The quiz is the last calibration before the capstone. The capstone is where the week's work becomes the artefact you carry out of C13.
