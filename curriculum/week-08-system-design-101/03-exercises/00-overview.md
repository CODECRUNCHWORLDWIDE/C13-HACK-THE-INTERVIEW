# Week 8 — Exercises

Three timed design rounds. Each runs the six-phase structure on a 45-minute clock against a real-sounding prompt, then has you diff your write-up against the answer key. The point isn't the "right" architecture — it's repping the navigation until the structure is automatic.

1. **[Exercise 1 — URL Shortener Walkthrough](./exercise-01-url-shortener-walkthrough.md)** — the canonical first design round; run all six phases end to end (~60 min + 15 min diff)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Rate Limiter](./exercise-02-rate-limiter.md)** — a tighter scope where the deep dive lives in the algorithm choice: fixed window vs. sliding window vs. token bucket (~60 min + 15 min diff)
3. **[Exercise 3 — News Feed](./exercise-03-news-feed.md)** — the big one; surface the celebrity problem in requirements and commit to a hybrid fan-out (~75 min + 15 min diff)

## How to work them

Do them in order — Exercise 1 establishes the six-phase muscle memory the other two assume. Run each on a real clock, out loud if you can, and produce the artefacts (diagrams, schema, numbers) the way your actual round will.

After each one, diff your write-up against **[SOLUTIONS.md](./SOLUTIONS.md)** — the answer key for all three. Don't read it first. The gap between what you produced under time pressure and what's in the key is exactly the feedback you came for; close it before moving to the next prompt.
