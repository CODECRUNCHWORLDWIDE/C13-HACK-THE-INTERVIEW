# Week 8 — Challenges

Two challenges. One inoculates you against the most common round-derailing event; one builds the rubric the interviewer is silently scoring you against.

1. **[Challenge 1 — The senior-drift drill](challenge-01-senior-drift.md)** — rehearse the four no-bluff response templates against five senior-level questions an interviewer pushes above your bar mid-round (~1 hr)
2. **[Challenge 2 — Build the rubric, score yourself](challenge-02-the-rubric.md)** — write the seven-dimension system-design rubric in your own words, then score one of your three exercise write-ups against it (~1 hr)

Both are optional but high-leverage, and both depend on the exercises and lectures being done first. Challenge 1 is composure: the single most-common derailing event for a new-grad or L4 in a design round is "senior drift" — a question above your level — and the strong move is never to bluff. The four templates (answer at your level / heard-of-but-can't-articulate / didn't-understand / above-my-level-full-stop) fit on a notecard you carry into the real round. Challenge 2 is calibration: you cannot see the company's rubric, so you build your own and silently score yourself at each phase boundary.

Challenge 2 hangs directly off the exercises — pick one of the URL shortener, rate limiter, or news-feed write-ups and score it across the seven dimensions, then carry the lowest-scoring dimension into a targeted drill before the real round. If you have a C13 peer also doing the exercises, swap rubrics and cross-score; the peer catches the No-Hires you rationalised into Lean Hires.

Run Challenge 1 first if a real design round is imminent (the no-bluff reflex is the one that fails live); run Challenge 2 first if you have time to calibrate before drilling. Both produce a small artefact — a notecard of templates, a one-page rubric — that you re-read the morning of the real round.
