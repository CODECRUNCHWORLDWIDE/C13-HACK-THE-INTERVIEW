## Tools you'll use this week

- **A recording app** — phone voice memo, Loom, QuickTime, or OBS. Audio is the minimum; video is better for the pair mock.
- **A stopwatch** — phone stopwatch is fine. The 4-6 minute recent-project answer and the conflict-story under 90 seconds both have timing components.
- **A peer who will play hiring manager for 30-45 minutes** — for Exercise 3 and the mini-project. Ideal: a C13 cohort member, a C2 peer, or a friend who has been a manager (current or former). Solo mode is available with the script provided.
- **A laptop with a markdown editor** — VS Code, Cursor, anything. The drafts live in your portfolio repo.
- **A notebook or a separate doc for note-taking during the call itself** — the HM screen is the first call where the *content* of your notes (what you wrote down about the team, the work) becomes useful in later stages.

## Glossary

| Term | One-line definition |
|------|---------------------|
| **Hiring manager (HM)** | The engineer or engineering leader who currently runs the team with the open role. They will be your direct manager if hired. |
| **HM screen** | The 30-45 minute second-stage call with the hiring manager, after the recruiter screen and before deeper technical rounds. |
| **The recent-project anchor** | The defining question of nearly every HM screen: some variant of "tell me about a recent project you worked on." The rest of the call orbits this answer. |
| **Drill-down** | The follow-up technique HMs use to test depth: "why did you choose X over Y?" → "what would have changed your mind?" → "did you consider Z?" Two layers deep is normal; three is a stress test. |
| **Ownership language** | "I" vs. "we." Strong candidates use both correctly; weak candidates hide individual decisions behind "the team did X." |
| **STAR / STAR+R** | Situation, Task, Action, Result, (Reflection). The structure HMs implicitly expect for any project or behavioural answer. |
| **The conflict question** | "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate / manager." Tests whether you can describe disagreement without burning a bridge. |
| **The weakness question** | "What's your biggest weakness?" Tests self-awareness; the worst answer is a humblebrag, the second-worst is a real weakness with no remediation. |
| **The first-90-days question** | "What would you do in the first 90 days in this role?" Tests whether you have actually thought about the role, not just the company. |
| **Manager README** | A short document a manager publishes about how they work — communication preferences, decision style, expectations. The closest public artifact to the rubric in an HM's head. |
| **Reverse interview** | The part of the HM screen where *you* are evaluating the manager. The closing 10 minutes, and the questions you ask, are scored on both sides. |
