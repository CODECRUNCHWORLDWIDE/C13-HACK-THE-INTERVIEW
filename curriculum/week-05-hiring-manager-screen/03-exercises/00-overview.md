# Week 5 — Exercises

Three exercises. Each operates on a different beat of the hiring manager screen.

1. **[Exercise 1 — Your recent-project story](./exercise-01-your-recent-project-story.md)** — draft, time, and rehearse the 4-6 minute recent-project answer until it survives two layers of drill-down (~90 min)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Questions to ask](./exercise-02-questions-to-ask.md)** — build your personal 12-question bank across the five categories, with three reusable defaults (~60 min)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Pair mock](./exercise-03-pair-mock.md)** — run a full 30-45 minute pair mock with a peer playing hiring manager, recorded and scored (~90 min including coordination)

## How to work them

Do them in order. Exercise 1 is load-bearing — the recent-project story is the anchor of the HM screen, and a strong story with weak drill-down survivability still fails. Exercise 2 builds the closing 10-15 minutes of the call, which is scored more heavily than candidates expect. Exercise 3 is where the first two get pressure-tested against a live partner.

**Don't claim HM-screen readiness until all three exercises are done.** A great recent-project story with no prepared questions back is a half-finished call. A great question bank but a vague project answer is the most-common failure mode. The combination is what wins.

If you cannot find a peer for Exercise 3, run it in solo mode with the recorded script — you'll lose the live-interruption rep, but the timed delivery and the self-review against the rubric still hold the value.
